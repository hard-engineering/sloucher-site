Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 8, 2026

Sloucher is a macOS menu-bar posture coach. It uses your Mac's camera to measure your posture on-device and nudge you when you start to slump. This policy explains exactly what Sloucher does and does not do with your information.

The short version: Sloucher processes camera frames locally on your Mac with Apple's Vision framework. It does not upload, collect, sell, or track your camera frames, posture measurements, settings, or any other data.

Camera

Sloucher uses the camera only to measure posture on your Mac. Camera frames are analyzed locally with Apple's Vision framework to estimate head and shoulder position relative to your calibration baseline. Camera frames are not uploaded, transmitted, or shared. Release builds of Sloucher do not write camera frame images or raw camera planes to disk.

Information stored on your device

Sloucher stores a small amount of settings data locally on your Mac (in macOS UserDefaults), including:

  • Calibration values from your upright baseline
  • Nudge preferences (such as sound and screen-glow settings)
  • Launch-at-login preference
  • Runtime diagnostic values used for local troubleshooting

This information stays on your Mac. It is not sent to us or to any third party.

Information we collect

None. Sloucher does not collect personal data, does not include analytics or advertising SDKs, and does not track you across apps or websites. The bundled Apple privacy manifest declares no collected data types and no tracking.

Third parties

Sloucher does not share data with third parties because it does not collect or transmit your data. The app runs entirely on your device.

Children's privacy

Sloucher is a general-audience utility and does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children.

Changes to this policy
