Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 9, 2026

sshhh is a macOS menu-bar dictation app. You hold the Option key to record speech; when you release it, sshhh transcribes the audio on your Mac and pastes the resulting text into the app you are using. This policy explains exactly what sshhh does and does not do with your information.

The short version: sshhh transcribes your speech locally on your Mac. It does not upload, collect, sell, or track your audio, transcripts, settings, or any other personal data. Your audio and transcripts never leave your device.

Microphone and audio

sshhh uses the microphone only while you hold the record key, to capture the speech you want transcribed. Audio is processed on your Mac and is converted to text locally using an on-device speech model. Audio is not uploaded, transmitted, or shared, and recorded audio is not saved to disk.

Transcript history stored on your device

So you can review what you have dictated, sshhh keeps a local history of your transcribed text on your Mac, stored in your Application Support folder (com.sshhh.app/transcription_history.json). This history stays on your device. It is not uploaded or shared, and you can delete individual entries or clear the entire history from within the app.

Accessibility and clipboard

sshhh uses macOS Accessibility permission to insert transcribed text into the active app by simulating a paste (Command-V), and it monitors the Option key system-wide so it can start and stop recording. sshhh does not log your keystrokes and does not read content from other apps. To insert text, sshhh places the transcribed text on the clipboard so it can be pasted into the app you are using.

Other information stored on your device

sshhh stores a small amount of data locally on your Mac, including:

  • Custom vocabulary and text-replacement entries you add to the Dictionary
  • App preferences (such as launch-at-login and widget settings)

This information stays on your Mac. It is not sent to us or to any third party.

Software update check

When it launches, sshhh asks GitHub's public releases API whether a newer version of the app is available, so it can let you know. This is a standard network request. It does not include your audio, transcripts, or any personal information.

Information we collect

None. sshhh does not collect personal data, does not include analytics or advertising SDKs, and does not track you across apps or websites. The bundled Apple privacy manifest declares no collected data types and no tracking.

Third parties

sshhh does not share your data with third parties. Aside from the optional update check described above, the app runs entirely on your device.

Children's privacy

sshhh is a general-audience utility and does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children.
